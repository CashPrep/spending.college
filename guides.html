College Money Guides

Simple, honest tips for saving money in college, finding jobs, and making your College Cash number work in real life.

Saving money in college

  • Lock in a weekly budget based on your College Cash monthly number.
  • Use your meal plan fully before eating out; treat restaurants as a treat, not default.
  • Buy textbooks used, rent, or share when possible instead of paying full price.
  • Stack student discounts on streaming, software, and subscriptions, or cancel what you barely use.
  • Walk, bike, or use campus transit when you can instead of rideshares.

Finding jobs in college

  • Start with campus jobs: library, front desk, tutoring, labs, or campus events office.
  • Check your school’s job board (Handshake, career center portal, or internal site) every week.
  • Look for remote flexible work: customer support, content editing, or online tutoring.
  • Use your major: labs, research assistant roles, or department office jobs pay and build your resume.
  • Match hours to your College Cash number using the Job Hours calculator.

Using your College Cash number

After you run the College Cash calculator, you get a monthly spending target. Use that number to decide how many hours to work and how strict your weekly budget needs to be.

  • Divide your monthly number by 4 to get a rough weekly spending limit.
  • Use the Job Hours tool to see how many hours you need at different hourly wages.
  • Review once a semester as your housing, meal plan, or lifestyle changes.
