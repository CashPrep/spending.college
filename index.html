About the data tap to expand

College Cash focuses on day-to-day spending money only — not tuition, not rent. The baseline is built from national “other expenses” data reported in recent college cost studies (transportation, personal, and miscellaneous categories) and typically lands around $2,400–$2,600 per year for a full-time student. [web:23][web:32]

We scale that baseline using each state’s cost-of-living index so that high-cost states show higher estimates than lower-cost states. This helps approximate how far the same amount of cash goes in different parts of the U.S. [web:23][web:32]

Lifestyle sliders and housing/meal choices then shift your estimate up or down. These adjustments are tuned to sit within realistic monthly spending bands reported in student spending research, but individual campuses and lifestyles can vary a lot. [web:24][web:27]

This tool is for planning only. It is not financial advice, and no estimate here is a guarantee of your actual future spending.
