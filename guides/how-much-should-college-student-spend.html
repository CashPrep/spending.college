How Much Should a College Student Spend Per Month?

Real averages based on 2025-26 student spending data

The typical college student living off-campus spends between $1,400 and $2,200 per month on living expenses, not counting tuition. Students living in dorms spend slightly less — around $1,200 to $1,800 per month — because housing and meal plans bundle costs and tend to run cheaper than market-rate apartments in expensive college towns.

Your actual number depends heavily on your state, housing situation, and lifestyle. A student in rural Ohio might spend $1,100/month total, while a student in Boston or San Francisco could hit $2,800/month with the same habits.

What Drives the Biggest Differences

  • State and city. Housing in California, New York, and Massachusetts costs 50–80% more than Texas, Ohio, or North Carolina.
  • On-campus vs. off-campus. Dorms often cost less than renting in expensive college towns, but off-campus can be cheaper if you share a 3-bedroom apartment with roommates.
  • Meal plan or cooking. Cooking your own food saves $100–300/month compared to dining halls or eating out regularly.
  • Car ownership. Students with cars spend $200–400/month more than those using bikes or public transit.

How to Know If You're Spending Too Much

  • Housing: 35–45% of your total budget. If it's over 50%, consider finding cheaper housing or getting more roommates.
  • Food: 20–30%. Above 35% usually means you're eating out too often.
  • Transportation: 5–15%. If it's over 20%, evaluate whether you really need a car.
  • Entertainment: 15–25%. Spending over 30% here is the easiest category to trim.

How to Lower Your Monthly Spending

  • Live with more roommates to split rent and utilities further
  • Cook meals in bulk and pack lunches instead of buying food on campus
  • Use student discounts for transportation, software, and entertainment
  • Buy used or rent textbooks instead of purchasing new ones
  • Limit subscriptions to 1–2 instead of stacking multiple streaming services
  • Walk, bike, or use campus shuttles instead of driving or using rideshares

Even small changes add up — cutting $10/day in unnecessary spending saves $300/month or $3,600/year.
