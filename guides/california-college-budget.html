California College Budget Guide

Complete cost breakdown for students in 2026

Planning your college budget for California? This guide breaks down what you'll actually spend as a student in Los Angeles and Berkeley and across the state. Whether you're living on campus or off, here's what to expect for tuition, housing, food, transportation, and everything else.

What to Know About California

  • UC and CSU systems offer relatively low in-state tuition
  • Housing costs vary dramatically by region - SF/LA much pricier than inland areas
  • Car ownership is common but public transit exists in major cities
  • Year-round warm weather reduces clothing and heating expenses

Money-Saving Tips for California Students

  • Apply for Cal Grant and state financial aid - California has generous programs
  • Consider living in shared apartments or co-ops near campus
  • Use Caltrain, BART, or Metro instead of owning a car in urban areas
  • Buy textbooks used or rent them - campus bookstores mark up heavily

How to Use This Budget

These figures are averages across public universities in California. Your actual costs will vary based on your specific school, lifestyle choices, and whether you qualify for financial aid or scholarships. Use this as a starting point to:

  • Estimate how much you'll need to save or borrow
  • Compare on-campus versus off-campus living costs
  • Plan monthly spending for food, transportation, and personal expenses
  • Identify areas where you can cut costs without sacrificing your college experience
