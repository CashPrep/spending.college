What to Know About Georgia

  • Georgia's HOPE Scholarship covers full tuition at public universities for students who graduate high school with a 3.0+ GPA — one of the best state merit aid programs in the country
  • Atlanta (Georgia Tech, Georgia State, Emory) has a booming tech and finance job market with strong internship pipelines
  • Athens (UGA) has a lower cost of living than Atlanta with a vibrant and affordable college-town atmosphere
  • Georgia has no state tax on student financial aid, keeping more of your aid money in your pocket

Money-Saving Tips for Georgia Students

  • Maintain your GPA to keep the HOPE Scholarship — losing it mid-college is one of the most common budget shocks Georgia students face
  • Georgia State's downtown Atlanta campus has strong co-op and internship pipelines that can offset tuition costs
  • MARTA (Atlanta's transit system) connects Georgia State and Georgia Tech to much of the metro area affordably
  • Athens off-campus housing is plentiful and reasonably priced — research neighborhoods north of campus for the best deals

How to Use This Budget

These are averages across public universities in Georgia. Your actual costs will vary based on your school, housing situation, and financial aid. Use this as a starting point to:

  • Estimate how much you need to save, borrow, or earn before and during school
  • Compare on-campus versus off-campus total costs side by side
  • Identify which expenses can realistically be reduced
  • Build a monthly spending plan once your housing and meal situation is confirmed
