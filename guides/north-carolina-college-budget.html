What to Know About North Carolina

  • UNC-Chapel Hill has one of the lowest in-state tuition rates among flagship public universities in the country
  • The Research Triangle (Raleigh-Durham-Chapel Hill) is a top tech and biotech hub with strong internship markets for students
  • Charlotte is one of the fastest-growing cities in the Southeast with a booming banking and finance job market near UNC Charlotte
  • Cost of living across NC is well below the national average, particularly for housing outside Chapel Hill and Charlotte

Money-Saving Tips for North Carolina Students

  • Apply for the NC Need-Based Scholarship — state aid for residents with financial need attending NC public universities
  • Chapel Hill and Carrboro have abundant off-campus housing; look east of campus on Franklin Street corridors for better deals
  • GoRaleigh and Chapel Hill Transit offer free or discounted student bus passes — check your school's transportation office
  • NC State has a strong co-op program in engineering and sciences — co-op earnings can significantly offset tuition costs

How to Use This Budget

These are averages across public universities in North Carolina. Your actual costs will vary based on your school, housing situation, and financial aid. Use this as a starting point to:

  • Estimate how much you need to save, borrow, or earn before and during school
  • Compare on-campus versus off-campus total costs side by side
  • Identify which expenses can realistically be reduced
  • Build a monthly spending plan once your housing and meal situation is confirmed
