Florida College Budget Guide

Complete cost breakdown for students in 2026

Planning your college budget for Florida? This guide breaks down what you'll actually spend as a student in Miami, Tampa, and Gainesville and across the state. Whether you're living on campus or off, here's what to expect for tuition, housing, food, transportation, and everything else.

What to Know About Florida

  • Among the lowest in-state tuition rates in the country
  • No state income tax benefits working students
  • Warm climate year-round reduces heating and winter clothing costs
  • Bright Futures scholarship program covers tuition for many FL residents

Money-Saving Tips for Florida Students

  • Qualify for Bright Futures - can cover 75-100% of tuition
  • Live off-campus in college towns like Gainesville or Tallahassee
  • Avoid owning a car if near campus - parking and insurance add up
  • Use prepaid college plans if your family enrolled you early

How to Use This Budget

These figures are averages across public universities in Florida. Your actual costs will vary based on your specific school, lifestyle choices, and whether you qualify for financial aid or scholarships. Use this as a starting point to:

  • Estimate how much you'll need to save or borrow
  • Compare on-campus versus off-campus living costs
  • Plan monthly spending for food, transportation, and personal expenses
  • Identify areas where you can cut costs without sacrificing your college experience
